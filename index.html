Pelé

A Football Legend

Pelé Over The Years

1940: Born Edson Arantes do Nascimento in Três Corações, Brazil.

1956: Debuted for the Santos football club after spending time in the junior team.

1958: Debuted for Brazil at the age of 17, and became the youngest to ever play in a World Cup Final. Pelé scored 2 goals in the final. Brazil beat Sweden 5-2 in this world cup, and he became famous for wearing the number 10 shirt.

1961: Pelé scored the 'Gol de Placa'- which means goal worthy of a plaque while playing for Santos against Fluminense. The goal was so amazing, a plaque was made to celebrate what is called the most beautiful goal in the history of football.

1962: Pelé and Brazil won the 1962 World Cup in Chile.

1969: Pelé scored his 1000th total career goal during a match against Vasco Da Gama.

1970: Pelé and Brazil won the 1962 World Cup in Mexico. This was Pelé's last world cup.

1971: Pelé played his last international game with Brazil against Yugoslavia.

1977: Pelé retires from football.

1992: Starting in 1992 Pelé was a United Nations ambassador for ecology and the environment. Since then he has been a Goodwill Ambassador and Brazil's Extraordinary Minister for Sport.

2012: Pele was awarded an honorary degree from the University of Edinburgh.
